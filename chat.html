Cryptography and Cryptanalysis

A structured overview of principles, techniques, and importance

What is Cryptography?

Definition: Ensures secure information storage and transmission using mathematical methods and algorithms.

Primary Goals:

  • Confidentiality
  • Integrity
  • Authentication
  • Non-repudiation

Types of Cryptography

  • Symmetric Cryptography
  • Asymmetric Cryptography

What is Cryptanalysis?

Definition: Uncovers weaknesses in cryptosystems, breaks them, decodes encrypted information, and evaluates security vulnerabilities.

Types of Cryptanalysis

  • Breaking Attacks
  • Analysis Attacks

Differences Between Cryptography and Cryptanalysis

Purpose:

  • Cryptography: Protect information
  • Cryptanalysis: Obtain protected information

Users:

  • Cryptography: Seek to protect
  • Cryptanalysis: Decipher protected

Methods:

  • Cryptography: Uses mathematical algorithms/protocols
  • Cryptanalysis: Exploits weaknesses in algorithms/protocols
